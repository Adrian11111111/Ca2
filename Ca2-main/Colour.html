Colour

Colour Provides Visual Cues

  • Colour will get your attention.
  • It will elicit an emotional response which suggests an associated meaning.
  • The right colours will draw the eye to the most important areas of the screen.

Colour implies meaning.

  • Colour is subjective. Our perceptions and biases affect what it says.
  • Some colours have universal appeal, colours are specific to geography, culture and experience.

Colour should highlight, not determine.

  • Colour should never be the sole differentiator of things.
  • It should highlight and guide.
  • For extended screen use and situations, use light, muted background colours.
  • Bright, saturated colours should only be used as visual accents.

The colour Wheel.

  • There are 3 primary colours: Blue, Yellow and Red.
  • There can be multiple secondary colours. You can get secondary colours by mixing two primary colours together.
  • Tertiary colours can be formed by mixing a primary colour by a secondary colour.
